Quick start checklist

  1. Create your account and sign in. If you’re new, start with a basic plan. Plans change — check current options on the official site.
  2. Define your objective: thumbnail, product mock, concept art, banner, or ad creative.
  3. Collect 3–5 references (brand colors, style cues, previous visuals) that represent your target look.
  4. Draft a first prompt that states subject, setting, mood, camera/lighting, and style notes.
  5. Generate small batches (few variations), review, and mark what works before moving on.
  6. Iterate on the best candidate: refine composition, mask regions on the canvas, and push details.
  7. Export in the right size/format. Keep a master version and web-optimized outputs.

Interface tour (at a glance)

Most AI image tools share familiar building blocks: a prompt area, model/style selectors, generation controls, a canvas/editor for targeted changes, and a gallery/history. Naming and exact features vary; use this mental model to stay oriented regardless of updates.

  • Prompt area: where you describe the scene and constraints.
  • Model/style: choose a general engine or a custom style you trained or unlocked.
  • Controls: resolution, guidance/strength, seed/variation, and batch size.
  • Canvas/workflow: mask regions, inpaint/outpaint, and schedule multi-step refinements.
  • Gallery/history: keep versions, compare, and restore earlier states.

Prompting fundamentals

Prompts are briefs. They guide composition and mood; they do not guarantee exact output. Keep them specific, testable, and free of ambiguous adjectives.

Structure your prompt

  • Subject & action: “ceramic coffee mug on walnut desk, top-down angle”.
  • Environment: “soft morning window light, blurred plant background”.
  • Style cues: “product photography, minimal, editorial, shallow depth of field”.
  • Color & materials: “matte white, subtle grain, warm tones”.
  • Quality intent: “clean edges, crisp details, no watermark”.

Example prompt: “ceramic coffee mug on walnut desk, top-down angle, soft morning window light, blurred plant background, product photography, minimal, shallow depth of field, matte white mug, warm tones, clean edges, crisp details, no text”.

Reference images & negatives

When available, add a reference image to anchor composition or texture. Pair it with negative prompts (undesired elements) such as “no text, no watermark, no extra hands, no frame”. Keep negatives short and relevant.

Iterate with intent

  • Change one thing at a time (lighting, camera angle, background).
  • Record what improved vs. what regressed (mini notes help).
  • Lock seeds/variations when you want to reproduce small changes around a baseline.

Style & consistency

Consistency usually requires constraints: repeatable prompts, controlled lighting, and, where offered, custom styles or trained models. Availability, limits, and costs may vary over time.

  1. Create a style sheet: document colors, materials, lenses, and adjectives you want to reuse.
  2. Use a stable subject description: e.g., “glossy black game controller with blue accent ring”.
  3. Lock composition hints: “45° three-quarter view, hero shot, centered, headroom preserved”.
  4. Leverage custom styles/models (if available): train with clean, representative examples; avoid mixed lighting or conflicting aesthetics.
  5. Version control: save successful prompts and seeds; duplicate before big changes.
Tip: batch-test 3–4 small variations per iteration. It’s faster to compare than to recall from memory.

Canvas & iterative workflows

Canvas tools let you fix issues locally instead of regenerating the whole image. Use masks to protect the good parts and force the model to rethink only the selected area.

  • Inpaint: mask a region (e.g., hand, logo area) and guide with a targeted sub-prompt.
  • Outpaint: extend the frame for banners or thumbnails while preserving scene logic.
  • Edge fixes: small masks along borders can remove artifacts from crops or upscales.
  • Multi-step: schedule subtle steps: background refinement → subject highlights → texture polish.

Keep masks feathered slightly to blend edges. Save intermediate versions to compare and roll back if needed.

Practical image controls

Exact control names differ by platform, but these knobs are common:

  • Guidance/strength: how closely the model follows your prompt. Too high can reduce variety; too low can drift.
  • Resolution & aspect ratio: start modest for speed, upscale later once composition works.
  • Seed/variations: lock for reproducibility; unlock to explore new branches.
  • Batch size: small batches (2–4) help compare outcomes without flooding your gallery.

When results feel “close but not quite,” nudge lighting and camera terms first (e.g., “soft rim light,” “35mm lens,” “eye-level”).

Upscaling & export

Work small to decide composition quickly, then upscale once you’re confident. Inspect edges, textures, and text-like details after upscaling; retouch via canvas if something breaks.

  • File formats: PNG for crisp assets, JPG for web with lightweight size; keep a master lossless copy.
  • Color: export sRGB for the web; confirm print specs with your printer (dpi, profile, bleed) if needed.
  • Metadata: store prompts/settings in a text note alongside the master export for future reuse.

Use-case templates

E-commerce product hero

Prompt: “stainless steel water bottle on soft gradient backdrop, three-quarter angle, studio lighting with subtle rim light, product photography, minimal, clean reflections, no label, no text, crisp details”.

  • Generate 3–4 variations to compare reflections and highlight roll-off.
  • Canvas: mask the label area to add/remove branding or tweak reflections.
  • Export: 1:1 for marketplaces, 4:5 for feed posts; keep background consistent across SKUs.

Game concept keyframe

Prompt: “ancient forest temple at dusk, volumetric light shafts, moss-covered stones, cinematic atmosphere, concept art, detailed foliage, dramatic sky, high dynamic range, moody”.

  • Lock seed to explore lighting tweaks without changing layout.
  • Canvas: inpaint focal statue details; outpaint to expand for banner crops.
  • Export: landscape 16:9; prepare secondary crops for presentation boards.

Marketing thumbnail

Prompt: “bold abstract shapes in brand colors, clean geometric background, high contrast, modern editorial style, no text, crisp edges, strong focal point”.

  • Iterate on color balance; keep empty space for overlay text in your editor.
  • Canvas: refine edges around the focal shape to avoid aliasing.
  • Export: 1280×720 or platform-specific dimensions; verify mobile legibility.

Troubleshooting guide

Banding or muddy lighting

  • Clarify light source: “soft studio key light with gentle rim light”.
  • Reduce conflicting adjectives (“dramatic & flat” together can confuse).

Hands, faces, or small objects look off

  • Zoom in with canvas and inpaint local fixes with concise sub-prompts.
  • Use references when allowed to anchor anatomy or props.

Text or logos distort

  • Keep “no text, no watermark” in negatives during generation.
  • Add final typography in a vector or photo editor after export.

Inconsistent style across a set

  • Reapply your style sheet; copy exact adjectives and camera/lighting cues.
  • Consider a custom style/model when available for tighter control.

Performance tips

  • Start with modest resolution for speed; upscale only your best results.
  • Batch small; delete obvious misses to keep the gallery tidy.
  • Save prompts and seeds in a note for reproducibility.
  • Use masks to fix issues surgically instead of regenerating from scratch.

Article FAQ

Is there a “best” prompt length?

No single length wins. Aim for clarity: subject, environment, lighting, style, and quality notes. Trim adjectives that do not affect composition.

Can I reuse the same seed to keep composition?

Seeds help reproduce variations around a baseline. Lock a seed when you like the framing and want to explore subtle changes.

How do I handle brand colors?

Include palette terms (“brand purple and cyan accents”) and keep a reference image when possible. Adjust saturation on export for exact matches.
